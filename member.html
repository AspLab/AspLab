Laboratory Member

Syu-Siang Wang, Ph.D

I received the B.S. (2008) and M.S. (2010) degrees in department of electrical engineering from National Changhua University of Education and National Chi Nan University, respectively. After one year service for military, I joined to the research group of Dr. Yu Tsao in the Research Center for Information Technology Innovation (CITI) at Academia Sinica till now. I am a PhD. student from 2013 and study in the department of Graduate Institute of Communication Engineering at National Taiwan University. Since I was studying the M.S. degree, discrete wavelet transform (DWT) was applied to extract robust speech features and to increase the recognized efficiency of an auto-speech recognition (ASR) system. Several researched results with extended works were published and won one excellent paper award at TAAI. In addition, I am involved in the research of speech synthesis and enhancement by applying nonnegative matrix factorizing (NMF) and deep neural network (DNN) to denoise the noisy speech wav file.

Lin, Tzu-Hao 林子皓, Ph.D

I received my Ph.D. from the Institute of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, National Taiwan University in 2013. My research interests focus on the application of machine learning in ecoacoustics and bioacoustics. During my Ph.D., I have developed several automatic detection and classification algorithms to search marine mammal vocalizations from long-duration underwater recordings. Recently, I focus on the analysis of soundscape. The soundscape contains information regarding the natural environment, biological activities, and human development. I have developed several blind source separation tools to facilitate the evaluation of soundscape structure and acoustic-based biodiversity assessment.
